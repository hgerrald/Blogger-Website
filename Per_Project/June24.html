Why do I doubt God? - Luke 1

Two angels appear in two distinct instances in Luke 1. In verse 11 an angel appears to Zechariah, a priest and the eventual father of John the Baptist, inside the temple of the Lord. On first sight, Zechariah is startled and gripped with fear, but the angel encourages him, “Do not be afraid. Your prayer has been heard”. The angel foretells how Zechariah and his wife Elizabeth will have a son, despite their old age, and that he will “be filled with the Holy Spirit even before he is born.” Despite Zechariah seeing this with his own eyes, he doubts what he sees and hears! He responds, “How can I be sure of this? I am an old man and my wife is well along in years.” This is a similar experience as found in Genesis with Abram and Sarah, but in contrast, “Abram believed the Lord, and he credited it to him as righteousness” (Genesis 15:6). Abram was unlike any other man in his time, his faith was incredible and the Lord blessed him tremendously.

The second angel appears to Mary, the eventual mother of Jesus. The angel explains to Mary that she will conceive and give birth to the Son of the Most High, but like Zechariah she too doubts! Mary asked the angel, “How will this be, since I am a virgin?”

How easy is it to doubt our God? Zechariah and Mary were both righteous people favored by God, and they had an angel of the Lord appear in front of their very eyes! Yet their natural response, despite seeing something this incredible, is to doubt. Does this not foretell that we too, having not seen such a phenomena with our earthly eyes, should struggle with this even more? When we do not have full trust in the Lord it becomes easy to doubt.
