Who created God?

If you spend some time to think about it, it’s actually a valid question. Who created God? If God created us, then wouldn’t something have to have created him?

In all honesty, the question just reinforces the fact the humans are drawn to a creator. We think all things must have a beginning because we had a beginning. In reality, we don’t have the correct perception of time; it’s like explaining color to a blind man, or pain to someone who feels nothing. We only know time relative to our experience, and our experience has a beginning.

From what we can see, humanity is the greatest thing this world has to offer. No other creation is nearly as intelligent. However, I would hate to actually believe this! Honestly, we are nowhere near as intelligent as some of us think. I watched a YouTube video once where a man drew a circle on a whiteboard and said it represented all of the knowledge the world had to offer. Everything, even above human understanding, belonged inside that circle. He then asked the hypothetical audience to shade in a correct amount representing how much of this knowledge they knew. To be honest, even if we took all of humanity, with knowing how vast this world is I doubt we could all even shade in half of it (and that’s extremely generous!). How much can a single person know of everything the world has to offer?

How much is really out there that we don’t even know about? We have senses for sight, hearing, feeling, taste, even somewhat of a sense of time. What if there is stuff out there which we don’t have a sense for? If we didn’t have ears would we know about sound? If we didn’t have eyes would we know about color? Would we be able to know about something even if we weren’t created to?

So in short, who created God?

Nobody created God! He is so far out of our understanding that it is difficult for us to comprehend how something can not have a beginning. Our lack of understanding of God is why we can’t ‘prove’ he exists. We just look to the evidence. Sure, something or someone could have created God, but there is no evidence for it so I will disregard it. Sure, Tupac could still be alive, but likewise where is the evidence? Now, although humans are not smart enough to pick up these senses of God, do we then conclude that he doesn’t exist? Do we disregard the writings of the early Christians, the life of Jesus, the fact that each of his disciples changed in such ways that they were martyred, murdered for the sake of the gospel?

I really don’t want to continue because I don’t want to be that guy, but I encourage you to think on your own of evidence of a God.

One last thought - humans really do have a sense for God, we just have to tune it to the right channel. Sin severs this sense. Have you ever felt Him calling you? Maybe when your heart is about to beat of your chest, or you are brought to tears by the Holy Spirit? He will reveal himself to you! Just make the effort and ask him!
